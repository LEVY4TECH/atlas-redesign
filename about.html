About Atlas

Atlas is a Kenyan property technology company leveraging on blockchain technology to digitize and safeguard land records through secure, tamper-proof digital archives

Atlas has a tripple mission: to secure property rights of bina fide land owners by preserving the integrity of critical land documents, to promote a seamless access to land records, and to deepen due diligence rigour in Kenya

By proactively using technology, Atlas aims to restore trust in Kenya's real estate market - a sector long plaqued by opacity and fraud

Atlas platform leverages the decentralization and immutabillity (tamper-proof nature) of blockchain to offer a more secure and accessible future for land ownership verification in Kenya and beyond.

As a proptech, Atlas is effectively an electronic land registry which is anchored in law under Land Redistration Act & Land Act 2012.

  • Real estate is one of the most opaque and analogue sectors in the world. It is especially worse in developing countries like Kenya with endemic corruption which makes it easier to forge title documents and disposses bona fide owners off their hard-earned assets
  • Atlas is keen to incorporate technology into this space for better service delivery

Atlas is a technology startup that is actively exploring ways to use blockchain technology to ensure data integrity. Our current area of focus is on the use of blockchain technology to secure the integrity of land records in Kenya. To this end, Atlas been building a blockchain powered platform that seeks to help property owners to digitize and secure their property records on the blockchain.

Atlas is banking on the decentralization (broad accessibility), immutability (tamper-proof nature) and security protocol of the blockchain to secure land records of people from all walks of life in Kenya and beyond. Atlas is an example of an electronic land registry, which is anchored in law under Land Registration Act & Land Act 2012.

“Our mission is aimed at improving land due diligence through technology, securing rights of bona fide landowners and restoring trust in the real estate sector in Kenya.”

  1. Promote access to land records.
  2. Sensitize the public on land matters, policies, procedures - because knowledge is power.
  3. Improve the due diligence experience & rigour on land for better decision support.
  4. Reinforce property (ownership) rights in Kenya.
  5. Improve transparency of land ownership
  6. Complement government land registry framework by offering an alternative point of reference for land
    records, as a redundancy to Government of Kenya registries.
